## Draft-and-queue probe (the retention question — answer per decision)
Pure advice doesn't retain; *completed work* does. So beyond "would it change my move," ask:
- Did I actually **execute** the recommended move within 72 hours? (Y/N)
- Would a **ready-made draft** — the landing copy / 5 interview questions / cold-DMs the harness would hand you on a scratch branch — have made me **more likely** to actually do it? (Y/N + why)

If "a ready-made draft would've moved me" is mostly Y, the **draft-and-queue thesis** holds on its own author — and that matters more for retention than whether the board beat one prompt.

## The pre-committed call (decide the kill line BEFORE you tally)
- **≥ 2 of 3 → BUILD v0.** The board earns its keep; you have proof (and a story for skeptics).
- **< 2 of 3 → STOP.** c-suite is "single advisor + red-team" at best — build *that* (it's a fraction
  of the work) and skip the board machinery. You learned this in a weekend, not a month.

Secondary signals worth noting (not the gate): did any option *flatter* or *overclaim about the
market*? That's the failure mode to watch — the board losing on this is more informative than winning.
